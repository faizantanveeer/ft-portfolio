Favourite
Tourist Spots

Pakistan

Hunza

Nestled in the Gilgit-Baltistan region, Hunza Valley is renowned for its breathtaking landscapes, including towering peaks like Rakaposhi and serene lakes such as Attabad. The valley offers a glimpse into the rich culture and hospitality of the local Hunza people.

Skardu

Skardu, a gateway to some of the world's highest peaks including K2, is famed for its stunning natural beauty with crystal-clear lakes, rugged mountains, and picturesque valleys. The enchanting Shangrila Resort, also known as "Heaven on Earth," is a must-visit.

Swat

Often referred to as the "Switzerland of the East," Swat Valley is known for its lush green valleys, scenic rivers, and historic Buddhist heritage sites. Malam Jabba, a popular ski resort in the valley, attracts tourists year-round.

Lahore Fort

Located in the heart of Lahore, these UNESCO World Heritage Sites showcase the grandeur of Mughal architecture. The fort and gardens offer a deep dive into Pakistan's rich history and cultural heritage.

Mohenjo-Daro

One of the largest settlements of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization, Mohenjo-Daro is an archaeological site in Sindh. It offers a fascinating glimpse into early urban planning and civilization, dating back to 2500 BCE.

Fairy Meadows

Situated at the base of Nanga Parbat, the ninth-highest mountain in the world, Fairy Meadows is known for its stunning views and lush alpine meadows. The area is a haven for trekkers and nature lovers seeking tranquility and adventure.
